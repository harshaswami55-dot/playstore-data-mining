DM Methodology
How 50+ users were identified

Audience identification

We used senior-likely signals, not verified age data.

What the data gives us

Google Play reviews include text, rating, date, app, language, and upvotes. They do not expose the reviewer's age, gender, or verified demographic profile.

So the 50+ segment is inferred from review language and senior-relevant pain points.

How we tagged senior-likely reviews

  • Explicit age and identity terms: senior citizen, elderly, retired, old age
  • Family terms: dadi, nana, dada, nani, grandfather, grandmother
  • Accessibility signals: small font, weak eyesight, spectacles, glasses
  • Motor-control signals: arthritis, shaky hands, big button, large text
  • Motivation signals: brain exercise, mental exercise, retirement boredom
Direct senior signal: keyword-matched reviews Proxy senior signal: readability, touch, language, offline, ads

DM Product Strategy
CEO decision frame

Recommended bet

Build a senior mode that feels premium even in the free version.

1

Accessibility baseline

Larger fonts, larger touch targets, high contrast, dark mode, and clear progress states. This is the product foundation, not a settings afterthought.

2

Respectful monetization

Reduce interruptive ads during active gameplay. Move monetization toward session breaks, rewards, and a simple ad-free upgrade.

3

India access layer

Hindi first, then regional-language expansion. Support offline play for weak data conditions and older phones.

Feature roadmap

  • Larger font and touch targets
  • Senior/easy mode with slower difficulty curve
  • Reduced or skippable ads
  • Hindi and regional-language UI
  • Offline mode for low-connectivity users
  • High-contrast and dark mode
  • Progress backup for device changes
Focus: trust, readability, continuity Outcome: higher retention and cleaner monetization
